Space exploration is the use of astronomy and space technology to explore outer space. While the study of space is carried out mainly by astronomers with telescopes, the physical exploration of space is conducted both by unmanned robotic space probes and human spaceflight.

The Space Age began in 1957 with the launch of Sputnik 1 by the Soviet Union. Since then, humanity has achieved significant milestones, including the Apollo moon landings, the Space Shuttle program, and the International Space Station (ISS).

Current trends in space exploration involve a mix of government agencies (like NASA, ESA, CNSA) and private companies (like SpaceX, Blue Origin). Goals include returning humans to the Moon (Artemis program), sending humans to Mars, and the search for extraterrestrial life.
